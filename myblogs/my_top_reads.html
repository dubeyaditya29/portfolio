-- I too had a love story --


I Too Had a Love Story pulls off an honest and poignant tale of love as Ravinder Singh sews a heart wrenching love story with his simple yet appealing words. By far this is my favorite book and it holds a very special place in my heart which I think no other book can ever take that

The novel is written in first person, where the author tries to describe the relationship between him and Khushi in such a manner that I couldn’t help but experience the entire thing through Ravin’s eyes. The book tells us the story of how two strangers with different goals and dreams in life met on a matrimonial site and how their love bloomed, until fate put it to its ultimate test

--The immortals of Meluha --


Imagine all the childhood stories, folklores, mythological characters and fantasies, you heard as a kid, getting manifested as a book? Well it would be called, “The Immortals of Meluha.” But what makes Immortals of Meluha an unique one? Its depth in the character, subtle message each scene carries secretly, transformation of characters, effective narration and no lag, brilliantly researched and written war strategies and lastly, the point of view, of protagonist

The story starts where Shiva, the head of tribe called Gunas, comes to the city of Meluha, as an immigrant. Meluha is an advanced city, where the people are immortals because of Somras, an elixir which keeps them alive. When Shiva enters the city of Meluha, the city of Meluha finds that Shiva is the saviour. Then the plot takes into different turns, where protagonists goes through a matrix of quests, answering all his unanswered questions. This book ends in beautiful note, and putting many unanswered questions once again, which are answered in the second book, Secret

--Rich Dad Poor Dad --


Robert Kiyosaki emphasizes six key points throughout the book. These points — which differentiate between his “poor” dad (his real dad) and the “rich” dad that helped him understand business and become wealthy — are: The rich don’t work for money, importance of financial literacy, minding your own business, taxes and corporations, the rich invent money and the need to work to learn and not to work for money. A great read though

The points which I specifically liked in this book are: the way he portrays the flawed educational system of our world, he proved how being an entrepreneur is less risky, our primary residence is not an asset and the way he explained about asset and liability.
